# V2 Product Brief

## Background

V1 successfully established the baseline playable game:

- Deterministic engine.
- Castle Wars-style rules.
- Original Magic & Mayhem-inspired retheme.
- Hot-seat and AI modes.
- Engine tests and production build.

However, V1 still feels like a functional card-game UI. V2 should make the experience easier to follow visually and more evocative of a strange wizard duel.

## V2 Player Promise

"I can watch the duel unfold on the table: cards leave the Portmanteau, spells appear in the center, Wards absorb blows, Citadels rise or crack, and I always understand what just happened."

## Core Experience Goals

1. **Visual clarity**
   - The player should understand the board without relying on the turn log.
   - The current turn, current actor, last cast card, Citadel, Ward, resources, and hands should be obvious.

2. **Physicality**
   - Cards should feel like objects moving between zones.
   - Citadel and Ward should feel like board pieces or magical structures, not just progress bars.
   - The Portmanteau should feel like a magical container that cards emerge from and return to.

3. **Magic & Mayhem-inspired atmosphere**
   - More handmade, mythic, strange, and old-PC-fantasy.
   - More creature/talisman identity.
   - More Law/Neutral/Chaos visual distinction.
   - No copied assets or branding.

4. **Pace**
   - Animations should improve comprehension, not slow the game.
   - V2 should still support fast play.
   - Reduced motion must remain available.

## Modes To Support

### Versus AI

- Human player at bottom.
- Human hand face up.
- AI hand face down.
- AI actions should be easy to follow through last-cast focus and board feedback.

### Local Secret Hot-Seat

- Active player hand face up.
- Inactive player hand hidden.
- Pass gate remains between turns.
- Board state, Citadel, Ward, resources, piles, and last cast remain visible.

### Local Open-Table

- Both hands visible.
- Useful for testing, casual local play, streaming, or learning.
- Should be selectable in setup or game options.

## Success Criteria

V2 succeeds if:

- A new player can finish a match while mostly following the visuals.
- It is clear where a card came from and where it went.
- It is clear which Citadel/Ward changed and why.
- The UI feels more like an original fantasy board game than a dashboard.
- The visual inspiration reads as "strange mythic wizard duel" without copying source games.
- Existing gameplay behavior remains intact.
